Signs of Gambling Problems

It’s important to recognize the signs of a potential gambling problem early. Some common warning signs include:

  • Spending more money or time on gambling than intended

  • Neglecting work, family, or social responsibilities

  • Chasing losses by continuing to gamble

  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble

  • Feeling anxious, guilty, or depressed about gambling

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, consider seeking help.

Safe Gambling Recommendations

We encourage all players to follow these safe gambling practices:

  • Set a budget and stick to it — never bet money you can’t afford to lose

  • Take regular breaks to keep a clear mind

  • Don’t gamble when under the influence of alcohol or drugs

  • Treat gambling as entertainment, not a way to make money

  • Keep track of time and spending while gambling

Responsible play ensures that gambling remains a fun and positive experience.

Aid Organizations and Support

If you or someone close to you is struggling with gambling, you are not alone. Independent organizations offer free and confidential advice, support, and counseling to those affected by gambling problems. We strongly encourage anyone in need to reach out to trusted support networks in their area.

Protection of Minors

We take the protection of minors very seriously. Gambling is strictly for individuals aged 18 and over. We implement age verification measures and recommend that parents and guardians take responsibility for preventing underage access to gambling.
